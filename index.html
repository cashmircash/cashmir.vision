Chain of Islamic Banking Around the World

1. CIS (Commonwealth of Independent States)

Islamic banking in the CIS is gradually developing, especially in Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. Kazakhstan is actively working on integrating Islamic financial institutions and creating favorable conditions for their operation. In Kyrgyzstan, work is also underway to introduce Islamic financial products.

In recent years, there has been growing interest in cryptocurrencies and blockchain technologies, which are seen as possible tools to improve the transparency and efficiency of Islamic finance. The government of Kazakhstan is already taking measures to adapt legislation to Islamic banking.

2. Central Asia

Central Asia, including Uzbekistan, Tajikistan, and Turkmenistan, is also beginning to show interest in Islamic financial instruments. Uzbekistan has started working on creating Islamic banks, while Tajikistan and Turkmenistan are exploring opportunities to attract Islamic capital.

The introduction of cryptocurrency tools and blockchain technologies is seen as a way to improve financial inclusion and simplify cross-border transfers.

3. Indonesia

Indonesia is the largest Muslim state in the world and is actively developing Islamic banking. Both state and private Islamic banks operate here, offering a wide range of products and services.

Indonesia is also actively working to improve the regulatory framework for Islamic banking. The introduction of crypto tools such as Islamic cryptocurrencies and tokens is becoming increasingly popular to support Islamic financial transactions and investments. In 2022, the volume of Islamic banking in Indonesia grew significantly, confirming the high interest in this segment.

4. MENA (Middle East and North Africa)

In MENA countries such as Saudi Arabia, UAE, Qatar, Kuwait, Egypt, and Morocco, Islamic banking occupies a significant place in the financial system. These countries have a developed infrastructure for Islamic finance and offer a variety of products, including Islamic bonds (sukuk), Islamic insurance (takaful), and others.

Some of these countries are beginning to experiment with blockchain technologies and cryptocurrencies to increase the efficiency and security of financial operations, as well as to create new Islamic financial products.

5. Mpesa

Mpesa is a popular mobile payment system in Kenya and other African countries. Although Mpesa is not an Islamic financial instrument, its successful mobile payment model can be adapted for Islamic finance, especially in microfinance and support for small businesses in Muslim countries.

The introduction of cryptocurrency solutions into the Mpesa system can enhance its flexibility and security, providing users with additional options for managing their finances.

6. London

London is one of the world's leading financial centers and is actively developing Islamic banking. Branches of major Islamic banks from MENA countries operate here, as well as specialized financial institutions offering Islamic financial products.

London also serves as a bridge between Islamic financial markets and Western investors. The introduction of crypto tools such as Islamic cryptocurrencies and tokens helps integrate Islamic financial products into global markets and attract new investments. In 2022, the volume of Islamic banking in the UK continued to grow.

7. Hawala System

Hawala is an ancient system of informal money transfers widely used in the Muslim world. The Hawala system is based on trust relationships between brokers (hawaladars) and allows for fast and efficient money transfers without using the formal banking system.

In some regions, especially in places with limited access to traditional banking services, Hawala continues to play an important role in financial operations. It can also be used in addition to formal Islamic financial instruments to facilitate money transfers and ensure financial inclusion. The introduction of blockchain technologies can enhance the transparency and security of Hawala operations while maintaining its traditional advantages.
